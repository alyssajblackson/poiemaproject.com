Founder

Alyssa
Blackson

Founder, Poiema Project

Alyssa Blackson is a student, advocate, and founder based in Birmingham, Alabama. She started Poiema Project after her own experience with undetected dorm mold left her navigating months of unexplained health symptoms and a healthcare system with no answers.

What began as a personal search for accountability became a statewide policy campaign, a publication, and a vision for what girls' wellness could look like when someone actually builds for it.

The Origin

Where it started and where it is going.

I came to college so excited. I unpacked my room, met my friends, and started something new. Three months later I had deep cystic acne, hair loss, brain fog, and a depression that felt physical, not emotional.

I spent the money I had saved for college on doctors chasing answers nobody could give me. It was mold. In the room I was paying to live in.

"I am not alone. And that is the whole point."

Nearly 40% of universities in the United States show documented signs of mold. Alabama ranks 4th in the nation for mold risk. And there are zero state laws requiring mold testing in student housing here.

Poiema Project exists because the gap between what students deserve and what they are actually given is too wide to ignore. The mold campaign is the first step. But it is not the last. This organization is built for the long arc of girls' health and wellness.

The Name

A word that holds everything.

Poiema is the Greek word for a work of art — something carefully and intentionally made. It is used in the New Testament to describe the way human beings are created: as masterworks, not accidents.

That is the lens this organization operates from. Every young woman we serve is a poiema. The environments she lives and learns in should reflect that.

What We Are Building

Three arms. One mission.

01

Policy Advocacy

The nonprofit arm. Starting with a statewide campaign for mandatory annual mold and moisture testing in Alabama university dormitories — and building from there toward a broader vision of healthy learning environments.

02

Rooted Rhythm

A publication for college-aged women. Real stories about health, identity, and the in-between. Delivered through Instagram and Substack to a community that deserves honest conversation.

03

What Comes Next

Poiema Project is in its first chapter. The policy work and the publication are the foundation. The broader vision for girls' wellness, health education, and systemic change is being built one step at a time.
